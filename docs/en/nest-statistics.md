# Statistics of Selected Nests

Xolmis Mobile provides a dedicated **Statistics** screen for analyzing multiple nests at once. This feature helps you understand breeding patterns, nest outcomes, and monitoring effort across different sites or time periods.

Statistics are available only when **two or more inactive nests** are selected.

## Accessing Nest Statistics

1. Open the **Nests** module.  
2. Switch to the **Inactive** tab.  
3. Select two or more nests using the checkboxes.  
4. Tap the **Statistics** button in the bottom bar.  

A new screen will open with summary information and visual charts.

## Summary Information

At the top of the statistics screen, you will find key metrics:

- **Selected nests** – total number of nests included in the analysis.  
- **Sampled localities** – number of distinct localities represented.  
- **Species richness** – number of species represented among the selected nests.  
- **Number of observers** – mean height of nests (if recorded).  
- **Apparent success rate** – rate of apparent nest success.
- **Nidoparasitism rate** – rate of nests with nidoparasitism.

These values provide a quick overview of the dataset.

## Nest Fate

A bar chart or summary table displays the final outcomes of the selected nests, such as:

- **Success**  
- **Lost**  
- **Unknown**  

This helps identify patterns in breeding success or failure across sites or species.

## When to Use Nest Statistics

Nest statistics are especially useful when:

- Comparing breeding success across species or sites  
- Evaluating monitoring effort in long-term studies  
- Identifying patterns in nest fates  
- Preparing reports or summaries for research projects  
- Reviewing data before exporting or importing into Xolmis Desktop  

These tools provide a quick, visual overview of your nest monitoring data and help guide further analysis.

---

The Statistics of Selected Nests feature gives you a powerful way to explore breeding biology data collected in the field, revealing trends and patterns that support ecological research and conservation work.
