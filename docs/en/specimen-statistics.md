# Statistics of Selected Specimens

Xolmis Mobile provides a **Statistics** screen for analyzing multiple specimens at once. This feature helps you understand collection patterns, species representation, locality distribution, and sampling effort across your fieldwork.

Statistics are available when **two or more specimens** are selected.

## Accessing Specimen Statistics

1. Open the **Specimens** module.  
2. Select two or more specimens using the checkboxes.  
3. Tap the **Statistics** button in the bottom bar.  

A new screen will open with summary information and visual charts.

## Summary Information

The statistics screen displays key metrics:

- **Selected specimens** – total number of specimens included.  
- **Localities sampled** – number of distinct localities.  
- **Species richness** – number of species represented.  
- **Total observers** – number of distinct observers.

## Sample Type Distribution

A pie or bar chart shows the proportion of different sample types, such as:

- Feathers  
- Tissue samples  
- Carcasses  
- Blood samples  
- Other materials  

This helps evaluate the diversity of collected material.

## When to Use Specimen Statistics

Specimen statistics are especially useful when:

- Reviewing field collection effort  
- Preparing reports or publications  
- Comparing sampling across species or sites  
- Identifying gaps in specimen coverage  
- Organizing data before exporting to Xolmis Desktop  

These tools provide a clear overview of your specimen collection and support more detailed analysis later.

---

The Statistics of Selected Specimens feature helps you visualize and understand your sampling patterns, ensuring that your biological collections are well-documented and scientifically valuable.
